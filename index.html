Lab data visualizations

Interactive explorers for Tasic 2018 mouse V1 GABAergic interneurons. Each cell type has four dual-panel viewers (cells on the left, genes on the right, hover to cross-recolour): the archetype tetrahedron (K=4 consensus NMF) and three recompute variants — SVD biplot, 3D UMAP, and diffusion map — that let you pick a subset of subtypes (or just the currently-visible genes) and refit the embedding in the browser. The SVD and UMAP recomputes also let you choose a lower rank. The SVD recompute adds a live variance-explained bar chart and a heatmap of cells × panel HVG ordered by pseudotime (PC1).

Source: Tasic et al. 2018, mouse VISp (anterior lateral motor + primary visual). Panel HVG selected per subclass on log-CPM via Seurat-v3 variance. Historically-flagged outlier subtypes (long-projecting Sst Chodl, chandelier Pvalb Vipr2, etc.) are dropped from the archetype NMF fit but included in the recompute variants — uncheck them in the subtype row and click recompute to drop them on demand. Hovering shows whether a gene is a panel HVG or NNLS-imputed from the broader transcriptome — imputed loadings for low-expression genes (most GPCRs) are noisy and should be read as rough estimates.
Built with Plotly · self-contained HTML, no server (UMAP recompute uses umap-js via CDN).
